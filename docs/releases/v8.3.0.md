# NOOP v8.3.0: a stronger Oura Ring 4 path

NOOP 8.3.0 substantially expands direct, local Oura Ring 4 support on Android and macOS. It can
adopt a factory-reset ring, reconnect with its stored application key, stream live physiology and
battery, and durably retain large overnight-history pages without a vendor account or cloud.

The support claim remains deliberately bounded. This release records strong evidence for one Ring 4
on Android and macOS; it does not claim every ring generation, firmware, or Apple platform is
supported.

## What changed

- **Local adoption and reconnect.** NOOP installs an application key only after an explicit adoption
  action and the ring's acknowledgement. The key is stored encrypted on-device. Later launches
  authenticate with that saved key instead of asking for another factory reset.
- **Live Ring 4 data.** Hardware runs verified live heart rate, R-R intervals, battery, a sustained
  worn session, and fresh-process reconnects. The shared Apple and Android interfaces now describe
  the connected Oura ring rather than showing WHOOP-only labels and controls.
- **Durable history sync.** NOOP carries the qualified UTC anchor and history cursor across
  reconnects, rejects unsafe or implausible time mappings, persists large pages in bounded,
  idempotent chunks, and advances the cursor only after every database write succeeds.
- **Qualified sleep windows.** Exact Ring 4 bedtime bounds can be stored as stage-less sleep
  sessions. NOOP does not invent stages when the tested history bank does not contain them.
- **Conservative blood oxygen.** The pipeline distinguishes percentage SpO2 from raw optical
  measurements, migrates older units safely, and calculates overnight values only from qualified
  percentages inside known sleep windows. If the ring emits no percentage record, the app displays
  no value.
- **Device-aware diagnostics.** The former Test Centre is focused on connected-device diagnostics,
  and Settings, Devices, Live, menus, and support surfaces use wearable-specific wording.
- **Maintenance.** Project contact now uses `noopapp@tuta.io`; retired Discord and Reddit links were
  removed from the README.

## Hardware evidence and limitations

The available Ring 4 (`ORE_06`, firmware `2.12.3`) passed factory-reset adoption, stored-key
authentication, battery, live HR/R-R, history, app relaunch, a sustained worn run, and large retained
history on Android and macOS. A retained page expanded to roughly 74,000 verified decoded events and
was committed before its cursor advanced. Overnight testing produced plausible stage-less sleep
windows and skin-temperature samples.

The tested banks did not contain a qualified SpO2 percentage or sleep-stage records, so 8.3.0 does
not display or infer those values. Reference-sensor comparison, a real out-of-range reconnect, a
full 24-hour run, an independent second-ring tuple, and physical iPhone testing remain. Ring 3 and
future ring generations are not covered by the Ring 4 result. See the
[hardware support matrix](https://github.com/neekolascmd/noop/blob/v8.3.0/docs/HARDWARE_SUPPORT.md)
for the full evidence and graduation gate.

## Downloads and platform limitations

All artifacts are built by GitHub Actions from tag `v8.3.0`. Verify the download using the attached
`SHA256SUMS.txt` before installing.

- **macOS — `NOOP-v8.3.0-macos.zip`.** Universal Apple Silicon and Intel application. The bundle is
  ad-hoc signed and not Apple-notarized because the project has no paid Developer ID. Gatekeeper may
  require Control-click → Open on first launch.
- **iOS — `NOOP-v8.3.0-ios.ipa`.** The IPA is unsigned and contains no embedded Watch app. Sign it
  with your own identity through AltStore, SideStore, or Xcode. The iOS target builds in CI, but
  direct Ring 4 BLE has not yet been verified on a physical iPhone.
- **Android — `NOOP-v8.3.0.apk`.** Sideload the full flavor on Android 8 or newer. It is signed with
  the continuation repository's stable release key. Certificate SHA-256:
  `15:0F:92:20:F6:58:1E:01:91:84:2C:B2:40:9B:34:50:EC:1D:DE:B6:36:64:46:A7:8B:15:89:56:51:FB:34:10`.
- **Unsupported platforms.** There is no supported Windows or Linux application in this release.

NOOP remains independent, experimental, local-first, and unaffiliated with WHOOP or Oura.
